Planning Your Job/Internship Search

Industry Recruiting Timeline

Recruiting timelines can vary widely by industry. While some employers—like large tech firms and consulting companies—participate in on-campus recruiting early in the academic year, many other fields such as libraries, archives, government, museums, and nonprofits may hire later or on a rolling basis. This means your job or internship search may involve a mix of campus recruiting, external job boards, company websites, and networking. Understanding these different timelines and how they relate to your chosen field will help you better plan your search and maximize your opportunities. UMSI CDO is here to support you through every stage of the process, regardless of where or when you look for positions.

  • Consulting & Financial Services:
    • Recruiting peaks in September - October
  • Consumer Goods & Services:
    • Recruiting peaks in data science, software development and some UX in September - October and continues throughout the winter semester, with more UX recruiting peaking in January - April.
  • Health & Medicine:
    • Recruiting peaks in January - May.
    • Health Administration Fellowship applications open in October.
  • Libraries & Archives:
    • Recruiting peaks in January - May
  • Technology:
    • Recruiting peaks in data science, software development, and some UX in September - October, and continues throughout the winter semester, with more UX recruiting peaking in January - April.
  • Nonprofits, startups, design agencies, & government:
    • Recruiting peaks in January - May.
    • Some government recruiting peaks in September - Oct for agencies requiring security clearance such as the CIA and FBI. Security clearance can take up to 3-6 months.

What can I do when applications are not as prevelant?

  • Network and connect with others.
  • Gain additional experience through projects, research, extra curriculars, etc.
  • Prepare your resume, cover letter, portfolio, and LinkedIn profile.

Finding Job/Internship Postings

There are several different places to search for job/internship postings. In this section, we'll highlight some of the most common places UMSI students find opportunities.

CareerLink

  • UMSI CareerLink offers on-demand access to career support: internships, jobs, career advising, career-building resources, special events and more. This online tool is exclusively for enrolled UMSI students and alumni.
  • Some of the most important features to know on CareerLink to help you search for job/internship postings include:
    • OCI and Job Listings Tab - This is the tab where you'll go to search for opportunities in CareerLink. OCI stands for on-campus interviews, and job listings are postings of opportunities that you can apply for.
    • Filters - We recommend using filters within OCI and job listings to streamline your search as much as possible. One of our favorite filters is the "Job Function" filter, which can be found by clicking "Add Filter" and then clicking "Job Function." The Job Function filter allows you to search by the type of work you want to do (i.e. UX Design, Library Science, Data Analytics, and/or Consulting).
    • "Turn on email alerts for this search" - You'll see this note in the bottom left corner of the OCI and Job Listings page. Click on it to set up automatic email alerts for when new opportunities that align with your filters are posted.

Job/Internship Search FAQ

  • How can networking help with my job or internship search?
    Networking is often the key to uncovering unposted or “hidden” opportunities. Reach out to UMSI alumni through LinkedIn, ask for introductions from faculty, and attend UMSI or industry networking events. Even short conversations can help expand your options and market yourself effectively.
  • How can I stand out in a competitive job or internship market?
    Tailor each application to match the job description, showcase relevant UMSI coursework, projects, and skills, build a strong digital presence, including an updated LinkedIn profile and online portfolio if applicable, prepare a compelling elevator pitch for networking situations, and practice interviewing with feedback from peers or career advisors.
  • What should my timeline look like for applying?
    Begin searching early, as some fields recruit months in advance (especially tech and consulting). For roles that hire later or on a rolling basis, check job boards frequently and be ready to act when an opportunity appears.
  • Are cover letters important for information jobs and internships?
    Yes—many organizations expect a thoughtful cover letter, especially for research, library, archive, or UX roles. Use your letter to explain your passion for information, showcase relevant skills, and demonstrate your knowledge of the employer’s mission or projects.
