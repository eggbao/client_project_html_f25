Networking and Building Connections

Making Connections

Building a professional network is the single most effective way to discover job and internship opportunities. Connecting with alumni can open doors to insider industry insights, company perspectives, and valuable advice tailored specifically for UMSI students. By reaching out and fostering relationships with professionals and fellow alumni, you’ll gain guidance on how to position yourself, strengthen your marketability, and confidently plan your next career steps. Whether you’re just starting at UMSI or looking to take the next leap, this section provides a few basic steps on how to find and make lasting connections.

  1. Find Alumni: Look into your network, both within UMSI and beyond it, for alumni that you could potentially network and connect with. This can include your friends, peers, mentors, supervisors, LinkedIn connections, and University of Michigan faculty. To find more people to network with, utilize the following CDO resources:
    • UMSI LinkedIn Group
    • UMSI Alumni Tool
    • CareerLink (employer contacts)
    • UCAN
    • Alumni Career Connections (see CareerLink for event details)
    • Networking or recruiting events (see CareerLink for event details)

  2. Reach Out & Get a Response: After finding someone you want to network with, now it is time to reach out to them through email. Each contact you connect with is an important and valuable addition to your network! Below are some pointers on what to do when writing your email:
    • Write a descriptive & enticing subject line.
    • Start with context that connects you.
    • Clearly state what you're looking for & why you’re reaching out. Do this by being specific on what your "ask" is, mentioning why you are reaching out to them in particular, and respecting their time by keeping your message short.
    • Establish a time frame for a chat with them. It is easier to say yes to 20 minute coffee chat in the next 2 weeks than an undefined time commitment.
    • Say thank you for their time!

Informational Interviews: Using the TIARA Method

The TIARA method is a framework for planning the questions you will ask in an informational interview. You can use this formula to gain information and establish a rapport.

  • Trends: This opening gives your contact time to reflect. Consider asking about a typical activity, the impact of their organization, and how their organization has changed.
  • Insights: Questions about their personal insights help you build a more personal connection with the contact. Ask your interviewee about lessons they've learned, what they enjoy about their work, or next steps in their career.
  • Advice: Asking for advice can help continue to build rapport and their comments could prove helpful for you later in the recruitment process. Consider asking questions about how you can succeed or what you can learn from their experience.
  • Resources: Does your contact have resources or tools they recommend for someone in your position? This question gives you reasons to follow up!
  • Assignments: Asking about recommended next steps can give you another reason to follow up with the contact. For example, "What kinds of projects seem to add the most value to your organization? I’d like to see if I can start doing that kind of work in my classes."

Networking FAQ

  • What if I’m an introvert or find networking uncomfortable?
    Start small and connect with other students or faculty, or attend group events. Preparing questions ahead of time and practicing your elevator pitch can boost your confidence. Remember, networking is a skill that improves with practice.
  • What if I don’t get a response?
    Don’t get discouraged. Response rates for cold messages can be low (around 20%). Try following up once if you don’t hear back after a week or two, or consider reaching out to others as well.
  • How early should I start networking as a UMSI student?
    It’s never too early! The sooner you start, the more confident and prepared you’ll be when searching for jobs or internships. Building genuine connections takes time.
  • How do I leverage career fairs or conferences for networking?
    Research attendees in advance, target specific organizations or alumni, and prepare a few talking points or questions. After meeting someone, follow up with a personalized thank-you message referencing your conversation.
